Writing

1. My Favorite Food

Write about your favorite food. Describe what it is, why you like it, and when you usually eat it.

2. A Recent Holiday

Write about a recent holiday you went on. Describe where you went, what you did, and how you felt.

3. A Person You Admire

Write about a person you admire. Explain who they are, why you admire them, and how they have influenced you.

4. Your Daily Routine

Write about your daily routine. Describe what you do in the morning, afternoon, and evening on a typical day.

5. A Special Event

Write about a special event you attended. Describe what the event was, who was there, and why it was special.

6. Your Best Friend

Write about your best friend. Describe their personality, what you like to do together, and why they are your best friend.

7. A Hobby You Enjoy

Write about a hobby you enjoy. Explain what the hobby is, how you started, and why you like it.

8. A Place You Would Like to Visit

Write about a place you would like to visit. Describe where it is, why you want to go there, and what you would do there.

9. Your Favorite Season

Write about your favorite season. Describe what the weather is like, what activities you enjoy during this season, and why it is your favorite.

10. A Memorable Experience

Write about a memorable experience you had. Describe what happened, how you felt, and why it was memorable.

11. Your School

Write about your school. Describe what it is like, what subjects you study, and what you like or dislike about it.

12. A Book You Have Read

Write about a book you have read. Describe the story, what you liked or disliked about it, and why you would or would not recommend it.

13. Your Ideal Weekend

Write about your ideal weekend. Describe what you would do, where you would go, and who you would spend it with.

14. A New Skill You Would Like to Learn

Write about a new skill you would like to learn. Describe what it is, why you want to learn it, and how you plan to do so.

15. A Pet You Would Like to Have

Write about a pet you would like to have. Describe what kind of pet it is, why you want it, and how you would take care of it.

16. A Dream Job

Write about your dream job. Describe what the job is, why you want to do it, and what skills or qualifications you need.

17. Your Favorite Place at Home

Write about your favorite place at home. Describe what it looks like, what you do there, and why it is your favorite place.

18. A Family Celebration

Write about a family celebration. Describe what the occasion was, how you celebrated, and what you enjoyed about it.

19. A Recent Movie

Write about a recent movie you watched. Describe the plot, what you liked or disliked about it, and whether you would recommend it.

20. A Local Event

Write about a local event that takes place in your town. Describe what the event is, when it happens, and what people do there.

Các thì

Simple Present Tense
Form Usage Example
Affirmative: Subject + verb (base form) + object Used for habitual actions or general truths. She reads a book every evening.
Negative: Subject + do/does not + verb (base form) + object Used to show something does not happen regularly. He does not like vegetables.
Question: Do/Does + subject + verb (base form) + object? Used to ask about regular activities or facts. Do you play soccer?
Simple Past Tense
Form Usage Example
Affirmative: Subject + verb (past form) + object Used for actions that happened at a specific time in the past. They visited the museum yesterday.
Negative: Subject + did not + verb (base form) + object Used to show an action did not happen in the past. She did not watch the movie last night.
Question: Did + subject + verb (base form) + object? Used to ask about actions that happened in the past. Did you enjoy the concert?
Present Continuous Tense
Form Usage Example
Affirmative: Subject + am/is/are + verb (-ing) + object Used for actions happening right now or ongoing actions. He is studying for his exams.
Negative: Subject + am/is/are not + verb (-ing) + object Used to show an action is not happening now. They are not playing outside today.
Question: Am/Is/Are + subject + verb (-ing) + object? Used to ask about ongoing actions. Are you coming to the party?
Future Simple Tense
Form Usage Example
Affirmative: Subject + will + verb (base form) + object Used for actions that will happen in the future. We will go to the beach next weekend.
Negative: Subject + will not + verb (base form) + object Used to show an action will not happen in the future. She will not attend the meeting tomorrow.
Question: Will + subject + verb (base form) + object? Used to ask about future actions. Will you visit your grandparents?
